Where it hurts today

Where the time really goes

Month-end logbooks are reconstructed from memory.

Vehicle tests, service and insurance are easily missed in Excel.

Manual trip classification creates time and tax risk.

How the agent works

Step by step

A controlled flow with driver review where needed.

Data collection

GPS, fuel-card transactions and driver calendars.

Trip assembly

Route, purpose and private or business classification.

Discrepancy checks

Off-route fuelling, unusual consumption and missing trips.

Deadline tracking

Tests, service, insurance and vignettes are flagged early.

Logbook output

Accounting-ready records; unclear trips and suspicious transactions go to a person.

What the agent does NOT do

Boundaries that build trust

Never on its own

It doesn’t decide on its own whether a trip is private or business when it’s unclear — it asks the driver.

Never on its own

It doesn’t delete or edit records retroactively without approval.

Never on its own

It doesn’t handle insurance claims — it only watches deadlines and warns you.

What we need from you

We keep your input to a minimum

  • Access to the GPS/fleet system, or at least the fuel cards.
  • A list of vehicles and drivers.
  • Rules for telling private and business trips apart.
