Vision 2030

A company that never sleeps. Where on that path are you?

Each agent is part of a larger operating layer: routine runs around the clock while people retain control. These five levels map the path to 2030.

Why now

The pace of change is accelerating

The range of tasks AI can handle on its own doubles roughly every 7 months.

Every agent therefore starts with a baseline, an owner and a defined benefit.

Five levels on the path

Where is your company on this path?

Use this as a quick orientation. Most companies begin at level 1 or 2, where progress can start fastest.

Manual operation

Data gets retyped between systems, knowledge lives in people's heads and inboxes.

Telltale sign: "that's something only Jarka in accounting knows."

Bought the tools, never measured the impact

AI licences exist, but regular use and measured adoption remain low.

Telltale sign: "we're paying for it, but we don't know whether it pays off."

People ready, first agents in production

Role-based adoption and the first production agents deliver measured hours, with people approving high-impact actions.

Telltale sign: “We can finally see its impact in the numbers.”

Connected agent workflows

Orders, warehouse and invoicing agents pass work between them; people review exceptions. One energy client operates 9 agents.

Telltale sign: "the agents pass work around on their own, we just approve the exceptions."

A company powered by the Loop (2030)

The agentic layer becomes core operations. Sensitive data and learned procedures stay inside; external tasks follow explicit rules.

Telltale sign: "we add a new process to the loop in days, not quarters."
