How the agent works

Step by step

A concrete flow — not promises. And just as important: what the agent deliberately does not do.

The employee requests

Wherever they’re used to — Teams, e-mail, phone. Just “I’d like leave 14–18 July.”

The agent checks

The balance, clashes in the team (who’s already off), and flags minimum staffing.

The manager approves

With a single click — they see the team calendar right there.

It records itself

Into attendance, the calendar and the payroll inputs.

The agent tracks balances, a person approves

It flags unused leave before year-end. But approval always rests with the manager — the agent enforces the rules and handles the admin.

What the agent does NOT do

Boundaries that build trust

Never on its own

It doesn’t approve a request on its own, even when it matches the rules.

Never on its own

It doesn’t rewrite the history of already-approved leave.

Never on its own

It doesn’t decide on exceptions beyond the rules.
