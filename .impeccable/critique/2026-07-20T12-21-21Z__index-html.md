## Anti-Patterns Verdict

The site passes the first-impression AI-slop test. It avoids glows, purple AI gradients, fake product mockups, decorative cursors and loading theatrics. The remaining risk is structural: repeated section kickers, numbered grids, ruled columns and similarly weighted card catalogues create familiar generated-landing-page grammar.

The deterministic scan found three signals across `index.html` and `reseni-faktury.html`: two `em-dash-overuse` warnings and one `numbered-section-markers` advisory. The four-step process numbering is a valid ordered sequence and therefore a false positive; decorative numbering across services and solution cards remains a credible design signal. Em-dash counts are editorial prompts rather than functional defects.

No user-visible detector overlay was injected because Assessment B had no browser surface. Parent browser verification did succeed: five representative routes rendered at desktop, the homepage and a solution page rendered at 390x844, no horizontal overflow or broken loaded image appeared, and the console contained no warnings or errors.

## Overall Impression

This is a good, modern and credible B2B technology site that can ship. The strongest opportunity is not another visual redesign. It is tightening the buyer journey and attaching verifiable proof to the strongest claims so the site feels uniquely EnterIT rather than a very polished category template.

## What's Working

- The hero is clear within seconds, the CTAs are visible in the first viewport, and desktop/mobile hierarchy remains intact.
- Real partner logos and real team photography create legitimacy without fake dashboards or stock AI imagery.
- The technical baseline is strong: 60 sitemap URLs return 200, 2,941 local references resolve, canonical/hreflang/OG/JSON-LD coverage is present, contrast tokens pass WCAG AA, focus styles and skip links exist, and reduced motion is handled.

## Priority Issues

### [P1] Precise results still require a trust leap

**Why it matters:** Anonymous case cards and headline metrics such as savings, conversion changes and payback periods are persuasive only if a risk-sensitive buyer can understand who measured them and how.

**Fix:** Publish at least one named or independently verifiable case study. Add timeframe, baseline, denominator and measurement method to the strongest metric. If client names cannot be public, use a redacted source artifact or an attributed quote.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable clarify`

### [P2] The homepage becomes a long proof plateau

**Why it matters:** Eight solutions, six results, architecture, process, integrations, operations, resources, calculator and team all compete before the final CTA. Mobile turns this into an especially long catalogue.

**Fix:** Keep three featured solutions and the strongest result in the primary journey. Move secondary proof behind dedicated pages or a deliberate disclosure. Bring security, ownership and low-risk pilot reassurance earlier.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable distill`

### [P2] Repeated section grammar limits brand distinctiveness

**Why it matters:** Kicker + oversized headline + ruled grid repeats often enough to become visible scaffolding rather than brand voice.

**Fix:** Remove most kickers, reserve numbers for true sequences, and give one proof section a unique EnterIT device based on a real artifact or named case rather than another equal card grid.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable layout`

### [P2] Motion is functional but not yet high-craft

**Why it matters:** The shared header entrance transition is overwritten by the later homepage `.nav-shell` rule, so it can jump. The mobile panel uses a 340ms keyframe entrance with an abrupt close, hover transforms are not consistently gated to fine pointers, and nearly every section receives the same 620ms reveal grammar.

**Fix:** Consolidate shell motion into one source, use interruptible sub-300ms UI transitions, gate hover movement with `(hover: hover) and (pointer: fine)`, and reserve scroll reveals for narrative peaks.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable animate`

### [P3] Small production optimizations remain

**Why it matters:** Two preloaded OTF fonts total roughly 127 KB; converting to WOFF2 would improve transfer cost. Several solution/legal meta descriptions are shorter than conventional search snippets, and shared shell rules are duplicated across CSS files.

**Fix:** Self-host WOFF2 variants, lengthen only commercially important short descriptions, and make `site-shell.css` the single source of navigation/footer rules.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable optimize`

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan, first-time buyer:** Eight equally weighted solutions and unexplained ERP/WMS/LLM/SLA terms make the right entry path less obvious. Anonymous results do not immediately answer whether this worked for a similar company.

**Riley, stress tester:** Precise metrics lack visible methodology. The booking CTA depends on an external Outlook flow without adjacent fallback guidance.

**Casey, distracted mobile visitor:** Touch targets are mostly sound, but a few controls measure 42-43px rather than 44px. The stacked solution/result/system catalogue creates scroll fatigue on a 390px viewport.

## Minor Observations

- CTA labels (`Probrat projekt`, `Probrat váš proces`, `Probrat moje procesy`) express nearly the same intent while routing differently.
- Homepage display tracking reaches approximately `-0.05em` in places; Czech and German headings would breathe better closer to `-0.03em` to `-0.04em`.
- FAQ answers are correctly visible directly on solution pages and FAQ structured data is present.
- A Lighthouse/Core Web Vitals measurement was unavailable locally, so the audit does not claim field CWV pass/fail.
